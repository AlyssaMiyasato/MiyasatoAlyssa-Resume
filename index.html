Alyssa Miyasato

Creative Writing

Alyssa Miyasato is a first-year student attending the University of California, Riverside and is majoring in Creative Writing and has an interest in Media and Cultural Studies. In Middle and High School, Alyssa participated in Color Guard and was the captain of her Middle School’s Colorguard team. Alyssa has great leadership experience and works well with others. In High School, Alyssa participated in Color Guard for 4 years and was in the Polynesian Cultural Club. Alyssa had to learn and master how to balance her extracurricular activities and her academic life. Alyssa was very successful in High School with her academics, being in her High School’s National Honor Society, and graduating with honors and a 4.0 GPA. In her Junior Year of High School, Alyssa had to pay herself for her own fair share, in order to continue to participate in Color Guard. Therefore over the Summer, she worked a part-time job at the Aquarium of the Pacific as a food runner. Every shift, Alyssa worked in a different position. She went from working at the coffee cart to being a prep cook to being a server at the restaurant. Alyssa has learned to quickly adapt to new surroundings, environments, and people, making her versatile. This experience has prepared Alyssa for her current part-time job.

Alyssa is currently working as a cashier at the fast-food restaurant Farmer Boys, and while pursuing her academic studies and being a full-time student. Alyssa has a background in customer service and foodservice. Alyssa has accounting, customer service, and leadership skills that have been developed while working in her current part-time job. Alyssa has worked with many diverse people and has an easy time making new acquantinces. She has resolved many conflicts with customers in a responsible, reasonable, and friendly fashion and makes it her responsibility to make the customer feel at ease and calm. She is very dedicated and hard-working and is always willing to help out. She helps her friends out whenever she can, covers her friend’s shifts, and stays longer at work to help her coworkers through the rushes.

Alyssa has always loved writing and wants to write stories that inspire others, especially Asian Americans. Alyssa has written pieces of poetry, nonfiction, and fiction that has sent and evaluated to different magazines in hopes to be published. Alyssa is also part of the University of California, Riverside’s Gamma Beta Phi National Honor Society for her exceptional academic standings. Alyssa has done hours of community service and maintains a high and stable GPA.
